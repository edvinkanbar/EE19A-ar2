About me

The best era yet

Born in an era where one gallon of gas was 91 cents and we still had to rewind our cassettes with wooden pencils, I grew up in a Swedish/Persian home where warm yellow-white rice with saffron was combined with minimalistic furniture, and the smell of indoor-smoking was lingering around from time to time.

Little did I know that I would grow up to become a multitalented writer & journalist, event planner & designer. But the road was long, and the bumps were too many to count.

It’s always easy to point fingers and blame others for the hard times you go through, but for me, the important part is where we pick up our broken pieces, glance at them and try to fit them back to where they belong in us.

During a career journey, this is truly important to remember, since most of us walk over our own pieces creating even tinier bits to put together before we climb even higher up the latter.

Writing as an escape

I still until today, remember the passion I had for writing and reading as a child. I was around 4 years old and remember climbing up in a tree with a pencil and a notepad, while watching the other kids below jumping up and down in puddles after the rain. I remember thinking to myself that I was lucky enough to know how to write, so I didn’t have the need to jump in puddles to have fun. The pen was my best friend back then, like the keyboard is today.

My Inspiration

I’m one of those awkward people you see walking on the street and stopping suddenly ( [from your point of view] – for no reason ) But I honestly am just looking at a smashed flower on the ground, seeing it as art. Wondering how many people smelled that particular flower, how it got crushed, and why its laying on the ground all by itself not being seen as “beautiful” anymore. If you ever watched the retro masterpiece “Amelie from Montmartre” – you’ll get the point.

As a really artistic person, I find it therapeutic to write no matter the subject, and I love to combine my writing and marketing skills to create memorable work for different audiences. I also fell in love with TV & media production after my internship at WUSA9 in the United States, after trying out life as a reporter in front of the camera.
